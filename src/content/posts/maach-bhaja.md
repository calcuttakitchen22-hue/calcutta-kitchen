## A Bengali Comfort Classic

Maach Bhaja is one of the foundations of Bengali cooking. While it looks deceptively simple, mastering it comes down to understanding heat, moisture, timing, and technique. This guide focuses on the small nuances that make the difference between broken, stuck fish and perfectly golden maach bhaja.


* Prep Time: 15 mins
* Cook Time: 15 mins
* Serves: 4

## Ingredients

- 500 g Rui or Katla fish, medium-thick Bengali cut
- 1 tsp salt
- 1–1.5 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tbsp mustard oil (for marination)
- 1/2 cup mustard oil (for frying)
- 1 onion, thinly sliced (optional)
- 2–3 green chillies, slit (optional)

## Method

1. Wash and thoroughly drain the fish
2. Marinate with salt, turmeric powder, and mustard oil for 10–15 minutes
3. Heat a round-bottom kadai until lightly smoking
4. Add mustard oil and heat until shimmering
5. Spread oil around the kadai using a khunti
6. Slide the fish gently into the oil instead of dropping it
7. Fry on medium-high heat for 2–3 minutes on the first side
8. Flip only when the fish naturally releases from the surface
9. Fry the second side for another 2–3 minutes until golden
10. For the optional finish, remove most of the oil and fry onions and green chillies until golden
11. Add the fish back and fry until extra crispy
12. Serve hot with steamed rice and dal

## Chef's Tips

- Never fry fish in a cold kadai
- Don't skip the mustard oil in the marinade
- Always drain excess water before marinating
- Slide the fish into the oil instead of dropping it
- Keep the flame slightly high to avoid temperature drops
- Let the fish release naturally before flipping
- A round-bottom kadai requires less oil and gives better results

## Frequently Asked Questions

- Why does fish stick to the kadai? because the kadai wasn't hot enough, the oil wasn't ready, or the fish was flipped too early
- Can I use a non-stick pan? Yes, but a traditional round-bottom kadai provides better frying results
- Why add mustard oil to the marinade? It helps control moisture, reduces splatter, and minimizes sticking
- When should I flip the fish? Only after it naturally releases from the surface and lifts easily with a spatula
